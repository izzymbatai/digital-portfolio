Purpose Statement

The first time I truly understood what a community could do for someone was in a classroom at Lakeside School. I vividly remember what educational environments felt like before Lakeside. I was often misunderstood, treated poorly, and felt like my potential was limited. At Lakeside, that all shifted. The community saw me differently. They challenged me, supported me, and introduced me to educators who reflected parts of my identity I had never seen in academic spaces before. That experience quietly reshaped how I saw myself and what I believed I could become.

Since then, I’ve been drawn to communities that give back in the same way. At Lakeside, I have returned the past two summers to lead their summer enrichment program aimed at serving Seattle Public School students. I also hope to return as a teacher and contribute to the same environment that transformed me. Beyond school, I have found similar meaning in community service. In my home church’s children’s ministry, where I have served for the past seven years, I’ve witnessed how consistent love builds confidence and belonging in children. At my local Boys & Girls Club, where I volunteered over 100 hours in high school, I saw how access to safe spaces, mentorship, and opportunity can change the trajectory of a young person’s life – because it first changed mine.

That sense of responsibility has extended beyond my immediate environment. During a service-learning trip to Nanuku, Fiji, I lived in a village where history is carried through oral storytelling. I came to understand how easily cultural knowledge can fade when it is not intentionally preserved. That realization taught me that it is not just about helping the communities that shaped me thrive in the present but also protecting the stories that define them forever.

A business education is a bridge between my purpose and long-term impact. It will equip me with the tools to organize ideas, build partnerships, and scale community-focused work in sustainable ways. It will teach me how to communicate and package meaningful initiatives so they can reach the people and resources needed to support them. Whether it is educational access, youth development, or cultural preservation, I want to help build systems that last for generations.

Ultimately, I hope to build a career centered on strengthening the communities that have shaped me while helping ensure that other people’s stories, like mine, are never lost in the process.
